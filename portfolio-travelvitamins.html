In 2009 I had the fortune to visit #Syria and its marvellous archaeological sites, rising up between stark blue sky and golden #desert. During Syrian Civil War many heritage sites and historic buildings in the country were damaged or even destroyed. In the list also #Apamea site, in the picture. Founded in 300 BC by #Seleucid kings, the city, located at a strategic crossroads for Eastern commerce, flourished for centuries. The city once boasted a monumental #colonnade and a Roman theatre. Today it seems not much is left, as the ancient city was looted by plunderers during the war.

Castel dell'ovo (litterally Egg Castle), a fortified castle in the bay of Naples, owes its name to roman poet Virgil. According to the legend, the poet hid a magical egg in the castle foundations, stating that the fate of the city of Naples was linked to the egg: as long as the egg (and the castle with it) remained intact the city would be spared from distruction and calamities. Snap from my last trip to this stunning city, November 2013.
